Slack redefined workplace communication, but its pricing, message history limits, and feature gaps leave room for alternatives. Whether you need better video conferencing, lower costs, or self-hosted control, there are compelling options available.

## Why look for a Slack alternative?

- **Pricing** — Slack's per-user pricing gets expensive as teams scale
- **Message history** — The free tier limits history to 90 days
- **Video conferencing** — Native video and audio capabilities are limited
- **Data control** — Some organizations require self-hosted communication tools
- **Feature overlap** — If you already pay for Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace, you may be doubling up

## Top alternatives

### 1. Microsoft Teams

**Best for:** Organizations already using Microsoft 365

Teams is the most direct Slack competitor and comes included with most Microsoft 365 plans. It offers robust messaging, excellent video conferencing, deep file integration through SharePoint, and tight connections with the entire Microsoft ecosystem. If you are already paying for Microsoft 365, switching to Teams can eliminate a significant line item.

**Pricing:** Free tier available. Included with Microsoft 365 Business Basic ($6/user/month).

### 2. Discord

**Best for:** Communities, startups, and informal teams

Discord started as a gaming communication platform but has become popular with developer communities, open-source projects, and startup teams. It offers excellent voice channels (always-on voice rooms you can drop into), strong community management tools, and a generous free tier. The interface feels more casual than Slack but is highly functional.

**Pricing:** Free with full features. Nitro at $10/month for enhanced uploads and streaming quality.

### 3. Rocket.Chat

**Best for:** Self-hosted and privacy-focused teams

Rocket.Chat is an open-source communication platform you can host on your own servers. It offers channels, direct messaging, video conferencing, and extensive customization. For organizations with strict data sovereignty requirements or those who want full control over their communication infrastructure, Rocket.Chat is the leading option.

**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted). Cloud plans start at $7/user/month.

### 4. Google Chat

**Best for:** Google Workspace users

Google Chat (integrated into Gmail and Google Workspace) provides straightforward team messaging with Spaces for group conversations. It integrates naturally with Google Docs, Drive, Meet, and Calendar. It lacks Slack's polish and integration depth, but if your organization runs on Google Workspace, it is already available and may be all you need.

**Pricing:** Included with Google Workspace plans starting at $7/user/month.

### 5. Element (Matrix)

**Best for:** Security-conscious and decentralized teams

Element is built on the Matrix protocol, an open standard for decentralized communication. It offers end-to-end encryption by default, federation between servers, and the ability to self-host. Governments and security-focused organizations use Element for its strong privacy guarantees.

**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted). Managed hosting starts at $5/user/month.

## Our recommendation

If you are on Microsoft 365, **Microsoft Teams** is the practical choice — it is likely already included in your plan. For communities and informal teams that want excellent voice features, **Discord** is surprisingly capable. If data ownership and privacy are non-negotiable, **Rocket.Chat** or **Element** give you full control with self-hosted deployments.
