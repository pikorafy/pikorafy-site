Trello's Kanban boards are beloved for their simplicity, but as teams and projects grow, many find themselves needing more. Whether you need better reporting, advanced automation, or a different approach to project management entirely, there are excellent Trello alternatives in 2026.

## Why look for a Trello alternative?

- **Limited views** — Free tier only offers the board view
- **Scalability** — Large projects with many cards become hard to manage
- **Reporting gaps** — Trello offers minimal analytics and progress tracking
- **Feature depth** — Dependencies, milestones, and workload management require workarounds
- **Pricing structure** — Advanced views and features require Standard or Premium plans

## Top alternatives

### 1. Asana

**Best for:** Teams managing complex, multi-stage projects

Asana is the most full-featured alternative to Trello. It provides list, board, timeline, and calendar views for every project, along with advanced features like task dependencies, milestones, goals, portfolios, and workload management. For teams that have outgrown Trello's simplicity, Asana offers the structure to manage real complexity.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 10 users. Starter plan at $13.49/user/month.

### 2. Linear

**Best for:** Software development teams

Linear has rapidly become the preferred project management tool for engineering teams. It is opinionated, fast, and built around modern software development workflows. Issues, cycles (sprints), roadmaps, and project tracking are all designed for speed. Linear's keyboard-first interface makes power users extremely productive.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 250 issues. Standard plan at $8/user/month.

### 3. Notion

**Best for:** Teams that want project management embedded in a knowledge base

Notion combines project management with documents, wikis, and databases. You can build Kanban boards, task lists, calendars, and custom views using its flexible database system. If your team's project management is tightly linked to documentation and knowledge sharing, Notion eliminates the need for separate tools.

**Pricing:** Free for individuals. Plus plan at $10/user/month.

### 4. ClickUp

**Best for:** Teams that want everything in one platform

ClickUp markets itself as the "everything app" for work, and it delivers an enormous feature set: tasks, docs, goals, whiteboards, time tracking, chat, and dashboards. It can replace not just Trello but several other tools as well. The trade-off is a steeper learning curve and an interface that can feel overwhelming at first.

**Pricing:** Free tier available. Unlimited plan at $7/user/month.

### 5. Monday.com

**Best for:** Non-technical teams and diverse workflows

Monday.com uses a colorful, spreadsheet-like interface that feels natural to teams coming from Excel or Google Sheets. It supports project management, CRM, marketing workflows, and more through customizable boards. Its visual approach to status tracking and reporting makes it accessible to teams across all departments.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 2 users. Basic plan at $12/seat/month.

## Our recommendation

If you are outgrowing Trello for general project management, **Asana** is the most natural upgrade — it has the depth Trello lacks while keeping a clean interface. Software teams should look at **Linear** for its speed and developer-focused design. If you want to consolidate project management and documentation, **Notion** is uniquely flexible. And if you want maximum features at a low price, **ClickUp** packs more into its free and low-cost tiers than any competitor.
