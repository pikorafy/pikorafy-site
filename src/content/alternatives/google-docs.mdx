Google Docs is the default cloud document editor for millions of people, but it is not without limitations. If you need better formatting control, offline reliability, privacy-focused alternatives, or a more powerful writing environment, several strong options are worth considering.

## Why look for a Google Docs alternative?

- **Limited formatting** — Google Docs offers basic formatting that falls short for complex documents
- **Google account required** — Full editing requires a Google account, which some collaborators may not have or want
- **Privacy concerns** — Google scans document content for various purposes
- **Offline limitations** — Offline mode requires Chrome and pre-setup, and can be unreliable
- **No advanced features** — Lacks built-in databases, knowledge management, or advanced writing tools

## Top alternatives

### 1. Notion

**Best for:** Teams that want documents, databases, and project management in one workspace

Notion goes far beyond document editing. Its block-based editor supports text, databases, kanban boards, calendars, and embedded content. For teams that need their documents connected to projects, wikis, and workflows, Notion replaces Google Docs and several other tools simultaneously. Real-time collaboration and commenting are built in.

**Pricing:** Free for personal use. Plus plan at $10/user/month. Business plan at $18/user/month.

### 2. Microsoft Word (Online and Desktop)

**Best for:** Users who need advanced document formatting and enterprise features

Microsoft Word remains the most powerful word processor available, with advanced formatting, styles, table of contents generation, mail merge, and track changes. Word Online provides free browser-based editing with real-time collaboration, while the desktop app offers the full feature set. For organizations on Microsoft 365, it integrates with Teams, OneDrive, and SharePoint.

**Pricing:** Word Online is free. Microsoft 365 Personal at $7/month. Microsoft 365 Business Basic at $6/user/month.

### 3. Craft

**Best for:** Mac and iOS users who want beautiful, structured documents

Craft offers a polished writing experience with nested pages, inline linking, and a focus on visual presentation. Documents look beautiful by default, and the native macOS and iOS apps are fast and responsive. Craft supports real-time collaboration and can export to Markdown, PDF, and other formats. It is particularly strong for documentation and knowledge sharing.

**Pricing:** Free for personal use with limits. Pro plan at $5/month. Business plan at $10/user/month.

### 4. Cryptpad

**Best for:** Privacy-focused users who want encrypted, open-source documents

CryptPad is an open-source, end-to-end encrypted office suite that includes documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and kanban boards. No account is required to use it, and the encryption means even the server operators cannot read your documents. It is ideal for journalists, activists, and anyone who needs private document collaboration.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 1 GB storage. Premium plans from $5/month for 25 GB.

### 5. Dropbox Paper

**Best for:** Dropbox users who want a simple collaborative writing tool

Dropbox Paper is a clean, minimal document editor built into Dropbox. It supports real-time collaboration, task assignments, timelines, and media embedding. While it lacks the formatting depth of Word or the flexibility of Notion, its simplicity is an advantage for teams that want a distraction-free writing space connected to their file storage.

**Pricing:** Included with Dropbox Plus ($12/month) and Business plans. Not available as a standalone product.

## Our recommendation

If you want more than just a document editor, **Notion** replaces Google Docs and adds databases, wikis, and project management in one workspace. If you need professional document formatting with track changes, **Microsoft Word** (especially Word Online for free) remains unmatched. For Apple users who value a beautiful writing experience, **Craft** delivers a polished, native app that makes documentation enjoyable. For maximum privacy, **CryptPad** offers encrypted collaboration that no other mainstream tool matches.
