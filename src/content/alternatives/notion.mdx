Notion is a powerhouse, but it is not for everyone. Whether you are concerned about performance, pricing, data ownership, or simply want a different approach, there are excellent alternatives worth considering.

## Why look for a Notion alternative?

- **Performance** — Notion can feel slow with large workspaces
- **Pricing** — Team plans add up quickly as your organization grows
- **Data ownership** — Your content lives in Notion's cloud
- **Complexity** — For simple note-taking, Notion can feel over-engineered

## Top alternatives

### 1. Obsidian

**Best for:** Individual knowledge management

Obsidian stores your notes as local Markdown files, giving you complete ownership of your data. It is blazing fast, highly extensible through community plugins, and supports powerful features like graph view and backlinks.

**Pricing:** Free for personal use. Sync ($4/mo) and Publish ($8/mo) are optional paid add-ons.

### 2. Coda

**Best for:** Teams that need powerful document-database hybrids

Coda combines the flexibility of documents with the power of spreadsheets and apps. It is particularly strong for teams that need custom workflows and automations built into their docs.

**Pricing:** Free tier available. Team plans start at $10/user/month.

### 3. Slite

**Best for:** Small teams that want simplicity

Slite focuses on team knowledge bases without the complexity of Notion. It includes AI-powered search and organization, making it easy to find information across your team's documents.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 50 documents. Standard plan at $8/user/month.

### 4. Craft

**Best for:** Apple ecosystem users who value beautiful design

Craft offers a native macOS and iOS experience with gorgeous typography and layout options. It is fast, supports real-time collaboration, and integrates well with Apple's ecosystem.

**Pricing:** Free tier available. Pro plan at $5/month.

## Our recommendation

If you are leaving Notion because of performance or data ownership concerns, **Obsidian** is the strongest alternative. If you need team collaboration features, **Coda** or **Slite** are better fits depending on your complexity needs.
