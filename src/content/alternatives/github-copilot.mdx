GitHub Copilot set the standard for AI-powered code completion, but the landscape has evolved considerably. Several alternatives now offer compelling features that may better fit your workflow.

## Why consider alternatives?

- **Cost** — Copilot's pricing may not suit individual developers or small teams
- **Privacy** — Some developers prefer tools that do not send code to external servers
- **Editor lock-in** — Copilot works best in VS Code; other editors get a lesser experience
- **Feature gaps** — Newer tools offer capabilities Copilot lacks

## Top alternatives

### 1. Cursor

**Best for:** Developers who want the deepest AI integration

Cursor is a fork of VS Code that rebuilds the editor around AI. Rather than adding AI as a plugin, every interaction — from editing to debugging to refactoring — is AI-native. Its multi-file editing capabilities are particularly strong.

**Pricing:** Free tier with limited usage. Pro at $20/month.

### 2. Codeium

**Best for:** Budget-conscious developers

Codeium offers a generous free tier with solid code completion and chat features. It supports a wide range of editors and languages, making it accessible to virtually any developer.

**Pricing:** Free for individuals. Teams plan at $12/user/month.

### 3. Tabnine

**Best for:** Enterprise teams with privacy requirements

Tabnine offers on-premise deployment options, making it the strongest choice for organizations that cannot send code to external servers. It supports custom model training on your codebase.

**Pricing:** Free tier available. Pro at $12/month. Enterprise pricing on request.

## Our recommendation

If you want the most powerful AI coding experience and are willing to switch editors, **Cursor** is the best option. If you want a free, capable tool that works in your existing editor, go with **Codeium**.
