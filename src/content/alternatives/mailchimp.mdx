Mailchimp is one of the most recognized names in email marketing, but its pricing has become aggressive, its free tier has shrunk, and its expanding feature set has added complexity that many users do not need. Whether you are a creator, small business, or growing startup, several alternatives offer better value for specific use cases.

## Why look for a Mailchimp alternative?

- **Rising costs** — Mailchimp's pricing scales steeply as your subscriber list grows
- **Duplicate contact billing** — Subscribers on multiple audiences count as separate contacts
- **Feature bloat** — Many users only need email but pay for a full marketing platform
- **Limited automation on lower tiers** — Advanced automation requires the Standard or Premium plan
- **Deliverability concerns** — Shared IP addresses on lower plans can affect inbox placement

## Top alternatives

### 1. ConvertKit (Kit)

**Best for:** Creators, bloggers, and digital product sellers

ConvertKit is built for people who build audiences through content. Its tag-based subscriber management means each person counts once regardless of how many sequences or tags they have. The visual automation builder is intuitive, and built-in tools for selling digital products and paid newsletters make it a complete creator platform.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 1,000 subscribers (limited features). Creator plan at $29/month for up to 1,000 subscribers with full automation.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for:** Budget-conscious businesses that send a lot of email

Brevo charges based on email volume rather than subscriber count, which makes it dramatically cheaper for businesses with large lists that send infrequently. It includes email, SMS, WhatsApp marketing, and a basic CRM in one platform. The free tier allows 300 emails per day with unlimited contacts.

**Pricing:** Free for 300 emails/day. Starter plan at $9/month for 5,000 emails/month. Business plan at $18/month for 5,000 emails with advanced features.

### 3. MailerLite

**Best for:** Small businesses that want simplicity and value

MailerLite offers a clean, straightforward email marketing experience without the bloat. Its drag-and-drop editor, landing page builder, and automation features cover what most small businesses need. The free tier is generous with up to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 emails per month.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 1,000 subscribers. Growing Business plan at $10/month for up to 500 subscribers, scaling with list size.

### 4. ActiveCampaign

**Best for:** Businesses that need advanced automation and CRM

ActiveCampaign combines powerful email marketing automation with a built-in CRM, making it ideal for sales-driven businesses. Its automation builder is one of the most capable on the market, supporting complex conditional workflows, lead scoring, and predictive sending. It is more expensive but significantly more powerful.

**Pricing:** Starter plan at $15/month for up to 1,000 contacts. Professional plan at $79/month with full automation and CRM.

### 5. Buttondown

**Best for:** Writers who want a simple, no-nonsense newsletter tool

Buttondown is a minimalist newsletter platform designed for writers who want to focus on writing, not marketing. It supports Markdown, has a clean subscriber management interface, and offers paid newsletter functionality. There is no drag-and-drop builder — just a text editor and a send button.

**Pricing:** Free for up to 100 subscribers. Basic plan at $9/month for up to 1,000 subscribers.

## Our recommendation

If you are a creator or blogger, **ConvertKit** is the best Mailchimp replacement with its subscriber-first approach and built-in commerce tools. If you have a large list but send infrequently, **Brevo's** volume-based pricing can save you significant money. For small businesses that want a clean, affordable tool without complexity, **MailerLite** delivers excellent value. If you need serious automation and sales pipeline integration, **ActiveCampaign** is worth the higher price.
