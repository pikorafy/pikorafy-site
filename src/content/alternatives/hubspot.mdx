HubSpot is one of the most capable all-in-one CRM and marketing platforms available, but it is also one of the most expensive as you scale. Its free tier is genuinely useful, but the jump to paid plans — and especially to the features needed for marketing automation — can be steep. Whether you are looking for a lower price, a more sales-focused tool, or a simpler setup, there are strong HubSpot alternatives to evaluate in 2026.

## Why look for a HubSpot alternative?

- **Cost at scale** — HubSpot's pricing grows significantly as your contact list grows and you add features
- **Sales-first needs** — Some teams want a pure CRM without HubSpot's marketing complexity
- **Feature depth in one area** — HubSpot is broad; specialized tools can be deeper in email marketing, sales automation, or customer service
- **Simplicity** — HubSpot can feel overwhelming for small businesses who only need basic CRM + email
- **Agency use** — Agencies managing multiple clients may find HubSpot's per-seat model expensive

## Top HubSpot alternatives

### 1. ActiveCampaign

**Best for: Email marketing and marketing automation**

ActiveCampaign is the most capable email marketing and automation platform available below enterprise pricing. Its visual automation builder lets you create sophisticated multi-step sequences that respond to contact behavior — link clicks, site visits, email opens, purchases — with far more granularity than HubSpot's starter plans allow.

ActiveCampaign includes a built-in CRM with sales automation, predictive lead scoring, and SMS marketing. Its email deliverability is excellent, and the reporting goes deep on campaign performance. For businesses that lead with email marketing and want CRM features to complement it, ActiveCampaign is often a better fit than HubSpot at a lower price point.

**Pricing:** From $15/month for 500 contacts. Scales by contact count.

**Best for:** E-commerce, course creators, coaches, and businesses where email automation is the primary use case.

### 2. Pipedrive

**Best for: Sales-focused teams who want a clean, fast CRM**

Pipedrive is a sales-first CRM built around the sales pipeline — deals move through stages, and the interface is designed to help sales reps stay focused on the right actions at the right time. It is significantly simpler than HubSpot's CRM, which is a feature rather than a limitation for sales teams who do not need the marketing complexity.

Pipedrive's AI Sales Assistant proactively suggests actions, highlights at-risk deals, and provides performance insights. The email integration is deep, with two-way sync and email templates built into the workflow. For teams of sales reps focused on closing deals, Pipedrive reduces friction compared to HubSpot.

**Pricing:** Essential plan from $14/seat/month. Advanced from $39/seat/month.

**Best for:** SMB sales teams, agencies, and B2B businesses that want a focused CRM without marketing overhead.

### 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

**Best for: Email + SMS marketing at lower cost**

Brevo is a marketing platform that provides email marketing, SMS campaigns, landing pages, and a CRM at a significantly lower price than HubSpot. It charges by emails sent rather than by contact count, which makes it much more affordable for businesses with large lists that they email infrequently.

Brevo's automation capabilities are solid, its transactional email delivery is excellent, and it includes WhatsApp marketing and push notifications on higher plans. For businesses in the mid-market looking for a HubSpot alternative with lower ongoing costs, Brevo is worth serious consideration.

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 emails/day. Starter from $25/month for 20,000 emails.

**Best for:** E-commerce, SaaS, and businesses with large contact lists that want lower per-email costs.

### 4. Salesforce (Starter Suite)

**Best for: Businesses planning to scale to enterprise CRM**

Salesforce is the enterprise CRM category leader, but its Starter Suite has brought an accessible entry point to the platform. Starting at $25/user/month, it includes CRM, email marketing, customer service tools, and basic automation — a HubSpot Starter equivalent.

The advantage of starting on Salesforce is the path to growth. When your business is ready for enterprise features — custom objects, complex workflow automation, AI forecasting, AppExchange integrations — Salesforce scales to that without a platform migration. HubSpot is also scalable, but Salesforce is the more established choice for complex enterprise requirements.

**Pricing:** Starter Suite from $25/user/month. Professional from $80/user/month.

**Best for:** Growing businesses that anticipate needing enterprise CRM capabilities, or those already in the Salesforce ecosystem.

### 5. Zoho CRM

**Best for: Value-focused businesses wanting a full feature set at lower cost**

Zoho CRM is one of the most feature-complete CRM platforms available at a mid-range price. It includes sales automation, workflow rules, AI predictions (Zia), multi-channel communication, and territory management at pricing that significantly undercuts HubSpot. Zoho's broader ecosystem (Zoho Books, Zoho Desk, Zoho Campaigns) provides an integrated suite similar to HubSpot at lower overall cost.

Zoho's interface is less polished than HubSpot's, and the learning curve is steeper. But for price-sensitive businesses that need feature depth, Zoho CRM consistently delivers strong value.

**Pricing:** Standard from $14/user/month. Professional from $23/user/month.

**Best for:** SMBs and growing businesses that want a full-featured CRM at lower cost than HubSpot.

### 6. Mailchimp

**Best for: Small businesses and creators who need email + basic CRM**

Mailchimp has expanded well beyond its email marketing origins and now includes a CRM, landing pages, basic automation, and social media posting. For small businesses that primarily need email marketing with light CRM functionality, Mailchimp's familiarity and free tier make it an easy entry point.

Mailchimp's automation is less powerful than ActiveCampaign, and its CRM is less capable than HubSpot or Pipedrive. But for small lists and simple workflows, it covers the fundamentals well.

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts. Essentials from $13/month.

**Best for:** Freelancers, small businesses, and creators who need simple email marketing with a minimal CRM.

## Our recommendation

If marketing automation is your primary use case, **ActiveCampaign** gives you more for less than HubSpot at comparable contact counts.

If you are sales-led and want a focused pipeline tool without marketing overhead, **Pipedrive** is the cleaner, faster option.

For growing businesses that want to stay affordable while building out marketing and sales processes, **Zoho CRM** or **Brevo** provide strong feature sets at lower cost.

HubSpot's real advantage is its ecosystem integration — when you use HubSpot for CRM, marketing, email, landing pages, and customer service, the data flow between tools is seamless. Alternatives often require integrating separate tools to match that breadth. But for teams who only need part of what HubSpot offers, paying for the full stack rarely makes sense.
