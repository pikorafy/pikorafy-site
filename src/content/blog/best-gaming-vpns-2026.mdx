Using a VPN for gaming sounds counterintuitive — adding an extra network hop should increase latency, not improve your experience. But in practice, a well-chosen gaming VPN can protect you from DDoS attacks, let you access game servers in other regions, bypass ISP throttling on gaming traffic, and occasionally even reduce ping by finding a more efficient route to the game server.

The key is choosing a VPN that minimizes the latency cost while delivering real benefits. Not every VPN is suited for gaming. Here are the ones that are.

## What to look for in a gaming VPN

**Latency impact** is the most critical factor. Every VPN adds some overhead, but the best gaming VPNs keep the increase under 5-10ms on nearby servers. A VPN that adds 50ms makes online gaming noticeably worse.

**Server locations** matter for two reasons. First, you want servers close to your actual location for minimal ping. Second, you want servers near game servers in other regions if you plan to play on foreign servers or access geo-restricted content.

**DDoS protection** is the primary security reason gamers use VPNs. In competitive gaming — especially in peer-to-peer games, tournaments, and streaming — exposing your IP address can lead to DDoS attacks that knock you offline. A VPN masks your real IP, and premium providers have infrastructure to absorb DDoS traffic.

**Protocol support** determines the balance between speed and security. WireGuard-based protocols (NordLynx, Lightway) offer the best performance for gaming because they are lightweight and fast. OpenVPN is more secure but adds more latency.

**Split tunneling** lets you route only your game traffic through the VPN while keeping other traffic on your normal connection. This is useful if you want DDoS protection for gaming but do not want to slow down background downloads or streaming.

## Gaming-specific use cases

### DDoS protection for competitive play

If you participate in competitive gaming, tournaments, or stream on Twitch, your IP address can be exposed through voice chat, peer-to-peer game connections, or even through Twitch itself. A VPN masks your real IP, and providers like NordVPN and ExpressVPN have infrastructure specifically designed to absorb volumetric DDoS attacks without disrupting your connection.

### Accessing regional game servers

Some games have better player populations or lower ping on servers in other regions. A VPN lets you connect to servers in Japan, Europe, South America, or wherever the player base is most active. CyberGhost and Surfshark are particularly good at this with their broad server networks.

### Bypassing ISP throttling

Some ISPs throttle gaming traffic during peak hours. A VPN encrypts your traffic so your ISP cannot identify it as gaming data and selectively slow it down. If you notice worse ping during evening hours that improves on a VPN, your ISP is likely throttling.

### Buying games at regional prices

Game prices vary by region on Steam, PlayStation Store, and other platforms. While using a VPN to buy games at lower regional prices technically violates most platform terms of service, it is a common practice. Surfshark and NordVPN are the most reliable for accessing different regional storefronts.

### Reducing ping (sometimes)

In rare cases, a VPN can actually reduce ping by routing traffic more efficiently than your ISP's default path. This typically happens when your ISP routes traffic through congested or geographically inefficient paths. Connecting to a VPN server near the game server can provide a more direct route. NordVPN and ExpressVPN are the best options to test this since their protocols add minimal overhead.

## The bottom line

For most gamers, **NordVPN** is the best choice. Its NordLynx protocol adds the least latency, the Meshnet feature is uniquely useful for gaming, and the overall package is strong across security, server coverage, and reliability. If budget is a priority, **Surfshark** offers excellent value with unlimited connections. For console gamers who need router-level VPN support, **ExpressVPN**'s router app is unmatched. **ProtonVPN** is the pick for privacy-first gamers willing to trade some performance for open-source transparency, and **CyberGhost** is best for geo-unblocking with its massive server network and gaming-labeled servers.

Whichever VPN you choose, always connect to the server closest to your game server — not the server closest to you — for the lowest possible ping.
