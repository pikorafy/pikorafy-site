## How to Set Up a VPN for Streaming

### Step 1: Choose and Subscribe
Pick a VPN (we recommend **NordVPN** for the best experience or **Surfshark** for the best value). Use the longest plan you are comfortable with for the best pricing.

### Step 2: Install the App
Download the VPN app on every device you stream on. Most VPNs have apps for Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, Fire TV Stick, and Android TV.

### Step 3: Connect to the Right Server
Select a server in the country whose content library you want to access. For US Netflix, connect to a US server. For BBC iPlayer, connect to a UK server.

### Step 4: Open Your Streaming App
Log into the streaming service as normal. The content library should reflect the country you are connected to.

### Troubleshooting Tips

- **Still seeing your local library?** Clear the streaming app's cache and cookies, or try a different server in the same country.
- **Getting a VPN proxy error?** Switch servers. Some get flagged by streaming services; others on the same VPN usually work.
- **Buffering?** Try a server closer to you, switch to WireGuard/NordLynx protocol, or use the smart DNS feature instead of the full VPN.
- **Not working on smart TV?** Use Smart DNS (available on NordVPN and Surfshark) or set up the VPN on your router.

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

Using a VPN is legal in most countries. However, accessing content through a VPN may violate streaming services' terms of service. While streaming services generally do not ban individual users for using VPNs (they simply block VPN IP addresses), it is worth being aware of.
