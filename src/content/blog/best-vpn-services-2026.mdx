VPNs have become essential infrastructure for anyone who values digital privacy, accesses geo-restricted content, or works remotely. But with dozens of providers competing for your subscription, choosing the right one requires knowing what actually matters — and what is just marketing noise.

## What makes a VPN worth paying for?

Before diving into specific providers, it helps to understand the criteria that separate good VPNs from great ones:

- **No-logs policy** — A credibly audited no-logs policy means the provider cannot hand over your browsing history even if compelled by law
- **Speed** — A slow VPN defeats the purpose for most real-world use cases
- **Server network** — More servers in more countries means better performance and more unblocking capability
- **Kill switch** — Cuts your internet if the VPN drops, preventing accidental IP exposure
- **Multi-device support** — You need coverage across phones, laptops, and sometimes routers
- **Obfuscation** — Ability to disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS, important in restrictive countries

## The top VPN services in 2026

### 1. NordVPN

**Best overall pick**

NordVPN remains the benchmark for consumer VPNs in 2026. Its combination of speed, server count (6,000+ in 111 countries), and security features puts it ahead of most competitors for everyday use.

Key features include Threat Protection Pro — a suite that blocks malware, trackers, and intrusive ads at the network level before they even reach your browser. The Meshnet feature lets you create a private encrypted network between your own devices and trusted contacts, which is genuinely useful for remote work and gaming.

NordVPN's no-logs policy has been independently audited multiple times, and the company is headquartered in Panama, outside 14 Eyes jurisdiction.

**Pricing:** From around $3.39/month on a 2-year plan. The standard plan covers 10 simultaneous devices.

**Best for:** Most users who want a fast, reliable, and private VPN without complexity.

### 2. ExpressVPN

**Best for speed and streaming**

ExpressVPN consistently scores at the top of speed benchmarks, making it the go-to choice for streaming 4K content or video calling over a VPN. Its Lightway protocol, developed in-house, provides excellent performance on mobile connections especially.

The service is available on virtually every platform including routers, smart TVs, and gaming consoles. ExpressVPN runs entirely in RAM — meaning no data is written to disk and everything is wiped on each reboot.

**Pricing:** From around $6.67/month on a 12-month plan. Covers 8 simultaneous devices.

**Best for:** Streaming enthusiasts and travelers who need reliable access to content libraries worldwide.

### 3. Surfshark

**Best budget option with no device limits**

Surfshark stands out by offering unlimited simultaneous connections — a major differentiator for households or small teams. Despite its lower price point, it does not cut corners on features: CleanWeb ad blocking, MultiHop double-VPN routing, and Nexus IP-rotation are all included.

The service has grown its server network significantly and now offers competitive speeds, though it still trails ExpressVPN on raw performance.

**Pricing:** From around $2.19/month on a 2-year plan. Unlimited devices.

**Best for:** Families, households, and small teams who want comprehensive coverage at a budget price.

### 4. Mullvad

**Best for serious privacy**

Mullvad takes privacy further than any other mainstream provider. It does not require an email address to sign up — you receive an account number and that is it. Payment can even be made with cash or cryptocurrency. Mullvad also charges a flat €5/month with no discounts, no lock-in, and no dark patterns.

The downside is that Mullvad prioritizes privacy over convenience — its app is more technical, and it does not have dedicated streaming servers. It also limits you to 5 simultaneous connections.

**Pricing:** €5/month flat. 5 simultaneous devices.

**Best for:** Privacy-focused users who want to minimize their data footprint with the provider itself.

### 5. Private Internet Access (PIA)

**Best for customization and open-source transparency**

PIA has one of the most customizable VPN apps available, with granular control over encryption settings, protocols, and DNS. Its open-source client code is publicly auditable, and its no-logs policy has been proven in court — twice.

PIA's server network is one of the largest available (35,000+ servers), and it offers 10 simultaneous connections on all plans.

**Pricing:** From around $2.03/month on a 3-year plan.

**Best for:** Technically inclined users who want transparency and maximum configurability.

## VPN use cases and which service fits

**Streaming Netflix, Disney+, and BBC iPlayer** → ExpressVPN is the most consistent at bypassing geo-restrictions across streaming platforms.

**Working remotely on public Wi-Fi** → NordVPN's Threat Protection and reliable kill switch make it ideal for protecting work connections.

**Family coverage on a budget** → Surfshark's unlimited device policy is the clear winner for households.

**Maximum anonymity** → Mullvad's no-account sign-up and flat-rate pricing minimizes your exposure even to the VPN provider.

**Developer and power user control** → PIA's open-source apps and granular settings give you the most visibility and control.

## What to avoid

Free VPNs are generally not worth the risk. Many free providers monetize by logging and selling your browsing data — the exact thing a VPN is supposed to prevent. If budget is a constraint, the budget tiers of paid providers like Surfshark and PIA are genuinely affordable and trustworthy.

Also be cautious of providers that make aggressive privacy claims without third-party audits to back them up. Words like "military-grade" are marketing, not technical specifications.

## The bottom line

For most people, **NordVPN** offers the best balance of speed, privacy, features, and price. If raw streaming performance matters most, choose **ExpressVPN**. For whole-household coverage at low cost, **Surfshark** wins. And if privacy is your primary concern and you want to minimize trust in any single party, **Mullvad** is in a class of its own.

A good VPN is a modest monthly expense that provides meaningful protection across all your devices. The question is not whether you need one — it is which one fits your workflow.
