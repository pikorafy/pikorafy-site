Wix and Squarespace are the two most popular website builders for people who want a professional site without writing code. Both offer drag-and-drop editors, templates, hosting, and e-commerce features, but they take fundamentally different approaches to design and flexibility. Here is how they stack up in 2026.

## Overview

**Wix** is a highly flexible website builder that gives users full creative control with its freeform drag-and-drop editor. It offers over 900 templates, an AI site builder, a large app marketplace, and tools for everything from simple portfolios to complex e-commerce stores. Wix serves over 250 million users worldwide.

**Squarespace** is a design-focused website builder known for its polished, professional templates and structured editing experience. It is the go-to choice for creatives, restaurants, and small businesses that want a beautiful site without wrestling with design decisions. Squarespace prioritizes aesthetics and consistency over total creative freedom.

## Key differences

### Design approach

Wix uses a freeform editor where you can place any element anywhere on the page. You have pixel-level control over positioning, sizing, and layering. This gives maximum creative freedom but also means you can create layouts that look uneven or break on different screen sizes if you are not careful.

Squarespace uses a structured grid-based editor. Elements snap into predefined sections and columns, ensuring consistent spacing and alignment. You have less creative freedom, but every layout looks polished and professional by default. Squarespace templates are widely regarded as some of the best-designed in the industry.

### Ease of use

Wix is easy to start with but can become complex. The freeform editor means more decisions, and the sheer number of features and apps can be overwhelming for beginners. Wix's AI site builder can generate a starter site from a few prompts, which helps with the initial setup.

Squarespace has a steeper initial learning curve because its structured editor works differently from what most people expect. However, once you understand the section-based approach, building pages is fast and consistent. There are fewer decisions to make, which many users find liberating.

### Templates

Wix offers over 900 templates across dozens of categories. Templates are highly customizable, and you can change nearly every aspect of a template after selecting it. However, you cannot switch templates after building your site without starting over.

Squarespace offers around 150 templates, but they are all built on the same underlying framework. This means you can switch templates at any time without losing your content. Every Squarespace template is responsive and professionally designed with careful attention to typography and spacing.

### E-commerce

Both platforms offer e-commerce capabilities, but they differ in scope. Squarespace includes e-commerce features on all plans, with full online store functionality on Business and Commerce plans. Its checkout experience is clean and conversion-optimized.

Wix offers e-commerce through dedicated Business and E-commerce plans. Its app marketplace adds features like dropshipping, print-on-demand, subscriptions, and multi-channel selling. For complex e-commerce needs, Wix offers more extensibility.

### SEO

Both platforms provide solid SEO foundations including custom meta titles, descriptions, alt text, URL slugs, and sitemaps. Wix has significantly improved its SEO capabilities and now includes a built-in SEO assistant. Squarespace's clean code structure and fast page loads give it a slight edge in technical SEO.

## Pros and cons

### Wix

**Pros:**
- Total creative freedom with freeform drag-and-drop editing
- Over 900 templates to choose from
- Large app marketplace extends functionality
- AI site builder generates a starting point quickly
- More flexible e-commerce with third-party app support
- Generous storage on higher plans

**Cons:**
- Freeform editing can produce inconsistent designs
- Cannot switch templates after building your site
- Site speed can suffer with too many apps installed
- Free plan includes Wix branding and ads
- Pricing tiers can be confusing
- Mobile editing requires separate optimization

### Squarespace

**Pros:**
- Best-in-class template designs with professional aesthetics
- Structured editor ensures consistent, polished layouts
- Can switch templates without losing content
- Unlimited storage on all plans
- Clean, conversion-focused e-commerce checkout
- Strong blogging tools built in

**Cons:**
- Less creative freedom than Wix
- Smaller template library
- No app marketplace for extending functionality
- Steeper initial learning curve
- E-commerce features are less flexible
- Third-party integrations are more limited

## When to use each

**Choose Wix if:**
- You want full creative control over your layout and design
- You need specific functionality from third-party apps
- You are building a complex site with unique requirements
- You want an AI-assisted site building experience
- You need advanced e-commerce features like dropshipping

**Choose Squarespace if:**
- Design quality is your top priority
- You want a professional-looking site with minimal effort
- You are a creative professional, artist, or photographer
- You prefer a structured, consistent editing experience
- You want unlimited storage without worrying about plan limits

## Verdict

**Squarespace** is the better choice for anyone who wants a beautiful, professional website without design expertise. Its templates are stunning, the structured editor prevents design mistakes, and unlimited storage simplifies planning. **Wix** is the better choice for users who want maximum flexibility, specific app integrations, or complex site requirements that benefit from its freeform approach.

For most small businesses, portfolios, and personal sites, Squarespace delivers a better end result with less effort. For users who need more customization or niche features, Wix's flexibility and app marketplace make it the more versatile platform.
