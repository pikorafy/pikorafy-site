ChatGPT and Claude are the two most capable general-purpose AI assistants available today. Both have improved dramatically, but they excel in different areas.

## Overview

**ChatGPT** (by OpenAI) popularized conversational AI and remains the most widely used AI assistant. It offers GPT-4o and o1 models with strong reasoning capabilities, web browsing, image generation, and a vast plugin ecosystem.

**Claude** (by Anthropic) has earned a reputation for nuanced, thoughtful responses and strong performance on complex reasoning and coding tasks. It emphasizes safety and offers an exceptionally large context window.

## Key differences

### Writing quality

Claude tends to produce more natural, less formulaic prose. ChatGPT is competent but can lean toward a recognizable "AI voice" if not prompted carefully. For long-form content, Claude generally requires fewer revisions.

### Coding ability

Both tools are strong at code generation. ChatGPT has broader plugin integrations, while Claude excels at understanding large codebases and providing detailed explanations of complex code.

### Context window

Claude offers a significantly larger context window (up to 200K tokens), making it better suited for tasks that involve long documents, large codebases, or extended conversations.

### Accuracy and hallucination

Both models can hallucinate, but Claude tends to be more conservative — it is more likely to say "I'm not sure" rather than fabricating an answer.

## Verdict

**Choose ChatGPT** if you want a Swiss-army-knife AI with web access, image generation, and plugin support.

**Choose Claude** if you prioritize writing quality, large-context tasks, coding assistance, or nuanced reasoning.

For many power users, maintaining subscriptions to both is worth the cost.
